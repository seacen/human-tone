# De-AI-Flavor · Minimal Rules (English)

**Principle: subtraction only.** If cutting an expression loses no meaning, cut it; if you can't cut, shorten it, restore the plain verb, or swap in the ordinary word. When unsure, leave it. A single occurrence isn't a crime — watch for clusters within a short passage that float free of the content and lose nothing when deleted. Rules run by pattern: diagnosis → fix, one example each, not an exhaustive list.

1. **Empty evaluation & inflation → cut.** Drop contentless praise: _it's worth noting, notably, undoubtedly, plays a crucial/pivotal role, stands as a testament to, in many ways._
   _e.g. "This undoubtedly marks a pivotal moment" → "This changes X" (or just cut it)._
2. **Unwarranted absolutes → downgrade or cut.** No _best / only / never / always / entirely_ without evidence in hand.
   _e.g. "the best solution ever built" → "the simpler solution here"._
3. **The antithesis mold → break.** "It's not just X, it's Y", "not merely… but rather…" props up cadence, not thought. State the point.
   _e.g. "It's not a setback, it's an opportunity" → "It's an opportunity."_
4. **Rule-of-three padding → trim.** If a third parallel item adds nothing, drop it; don't keep it for rhythm.
5. **Narrator scaffolding → cut.** Throat-clearing goes: _"Let's dive in", "In this section", "It's important to remember", "First… Second… Finally."_ Get to the point.
6. **Hedging → drop.** _"may potentially", "it could be argued that", "generally speaking", "some would say", reflexive "arguably."_ Keep only when it carries real uncertainty.
7. **Verbal false limbs → restore the verb.** _"make a decision" → "decide", "conduct an analysis" → "analyze", "provide assistance" → "help", "give consideration to" → "consider."_
8. **Nominalization & reflexive passive → verb, active.** _"the implementation of the fix was carried out" → "we fixed it", "there was a reduction in errors" → "errors dropped."_
9. **The GPT lexicon & pretentious diction → plain words.** _delve, tapestry, testament, realm, landscape (fig.), leverage, robust, seamless, foster, underscore, showcase, elevate, navigate (fig.), intricate, meticulous_ → the ordinary word.
10. **Dead metaphors & reified abstractions → state it literally.** Clichés: _"move the needle" → "make a real difference", "low-hanging fruit" → "the easy wins", "circle back" → "follow up."_ And don't let abstractions physically act: _ideas take root, trust grows into, hold space for, prop up, carry the weight of_ → say the relation plainly.
11. **Metaphor abuse → thin it out; make coinages literal.** Piling two or three images in one breath — cut to one. A coinage you have to decode ("welding a centuries-old seam") → say what you actually mean.
12. **Suspense-then-reveal → give the conclusion.** _"Here's the thing…", "The truth is…", "What most people miss is…"_ — drop the tease, state it.
13. **Staccato drama & em-dash tics → rejoin.** Fragments and dashes inserted for a beat — remove when they carry no content. _"We won. Completely. No contest." → "We won cleanly."_
14. **Placeholder vagueness → name it.** Vague _"this", "a certain kind of", "something", "there's a magic to it"_ standing in for a nameable thing — name the thing.
   _e.g. "there's a certain something that makes it work" → "repeat purchases make it work"._
15. **Empty category shell → drop the shell.** _"the whole X thing", "X as a concept", "X as an entity"_ around a clean noun — cut the wrapper.
   _e.g. "the whole company thing is basically…" → "a company is basically…"._
16. **Summary bookending → cut.** _"In today's fast-paced world…", "At the end of the day…", "In conclusion, …."_

**Don't touch (guardrails — not AI-speak):**
- Terms, acronyms (RPI / SKU / NRR…), product/proper names, ingredient names.
- Quotes, code, commands, numbers, dates, versions — verbatim.
- Legit register: legal / academic / technical writing where high nominalization or hedging is the norm ("pursuant to", "we hypothesize that" — normal there, not a tell).
- Intentional rhetoric: a speech's rule-of-three, a deliberate refrain, poetry.
- A real person's casual, uneven, first-draft voice — raise the bar; lean toward leaving it.

**One ruler:** don't flatten the prose into beige, uniform, manual-like text — that's its own failure. When in doubt, under-cut rather than mis-cut — erasing a real author's judgment, tone, and detail is less reversible than a little leftover AI tone.
